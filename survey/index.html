Evaluating LACK

A Database of Climate Lobbying Networks

Expert survey - takes 5 to 7 minutes

Who we are: This survey is conducted by the ClimateSense project, a European research initiative (CHIST-ERA, 2025-2028) that develops tools to monitor and analyse climate misinformation. The project brings together researchers from the Open University (UK), EURECOM (France), Prague University of Economics and Business (Czech Republic), and Kaunas University of Technology (Lithuania).

As part of ClimateSense, we are building a new resource called LACK (Lobbying Against Climate Knowledge) and would like your expert opinion on whether it captures the right information and could be useful in practice.

What is LACK?

LACK is a searchable database that maps the people and organisations involved in lobbying against climate science and climate policy. It brings together information from two well-known investigative sources:

  • DeSmog Climate Disinformation Database, which profiles individuals and organisations involved in climate disinformation (943 profile pages)
  • InfluenceMap / LobbyMap, which tracks how companies and industry groups engage with climate policy (1,302 entries)

Instead of reading through hundreds of separate profile pages across different websites, LACK allows you to search and explore the connections between actors in one place.

How LACK works: combining fragmented sources into one searchable database

Every connection links back to the original source page it was extracted from, so users can always verify the information. The database covers the period from 1905 to 2030.

What kind of questions can you ask?

"Who funds the Heartland Institute, and where does their money come from?"
"Which people have worked at both a fossil fuel company and a government advisory body?"
"Show me all organisations funded by DonorsTrust."
"Which directors of think tank X also sit on the board of company Y?"
"What organisations were created after 2015 and are no longer active?"
"Who are the most connected people in the network?"
"Trace the path from Koch Industries to a specific public-facing advocacy group."
Example: searching for "Center for Truth in Science" reveals funders, directors, and their other affiliations

This is one search. Each of these actors can then be explored further, revealing more connections and building a picture of the full network.
